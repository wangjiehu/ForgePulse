# SOP: Drive System and Web Tension Faults

## DRV-410 Drive Current Above Normal Range

Meaning: the drive motor current has exceeded the normal operating band, indicating increased mechanical load or resistance.

Recommended checks:

1. Check roller surface condition for contamination, wear, or material buildup.
2. Inspect drive belt tension and alignment.
3. Check bearing condition and lubrication.
4. Verify no foreign object or wrapped material on rollers.
5. If current remains elevated, schedule controlled stop for roller inspection and cleaning.

Safety note: do not bypass overcurrent protection. Elevated current may indicate mechanical binding.

## TEN-204 Web Tension Oscillation

Meaning: web tension fluctuation exceeds the warning band and may affect coating uniformity.

Recommended checks:

1. Check dancer roller position feedback.
2. Check tension controller response after recipe switch.
3. Inspect drive current trend and roller surface contamination.
4. Reduce line speed only after process engineer approval.

## QCS-318 Thickness Drift

Meaning: inline inspection detected coating thickness drifting away from target band.

Recommended checks:

1. Correlate drift time with temperature, tension and line speed changes.
2. Isolate affected material roll range.
3. Request quality engineer review before release.

## Roller Contamination Response

When DRV-410 and TEN-204 occur together:

1. Prioritize roller surface inspection – contamination can cause both elevated drive current and tension instability.
2. Check if recent coating material change or process adjustment increased residue buildup.
3. Schedule roller cleaning during next planned stop if immediate stop is not warranted.
4. Document affected material roll range for quality review.
